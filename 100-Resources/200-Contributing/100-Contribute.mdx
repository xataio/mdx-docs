Xata is an open and collaborative platform, and there are numerous ways for you to contribute and get involved in the platforms development.

## Join the community

Connect with fellow developers using Xata and those who know the product on [Discord](https://xata.io/discord). The Xata Discord is ideal for discussing Xata, development practices, and documentation-related topics; join to engage and be a part of our vibrant community.

## Feature requests

Your feedback and ideas are essential for improving Xata. If you have suggestions for new features or enhancements, share them with us. Your input plays a crucial role in shaping the future of Xata.

You can open a feature request at https://feedback.xata.io/feature-requests. Provide a title and a detailed description of the feature you're interested in. After filling in the details, click **Create Post** to submit your request. You have the option to support requests made by others by upvoting any existing post or the one you've just created.

### OpenAPI specification

Our API is defined via [OpenAPI specifications](https://github.com/OAI/OpenAPI-Specification). For a comprehensive understanding and further details on these specifications, refer to the [documentation](/docs/rest-api/contexts).
You can pull the [OpenAPI specifications](/docs/rest-api/contexts#openapi-specifications) or go to the [REST API](/docs/api-reference/user#get-user-details) for more information.

### Issues and labels

To help you get started, we have labeled issues as "**good first issue**" for newcomers and "**help wanted**" for tasks of varying complexity. These labels serve as guides to find suitable tasks. Comment on any issue that is of interest and contact us on Discord to learn more about the context.

### Report bugs

Bug reports help us enhance the platform and are greatly appreciated. If you encounter any issues, report them by opening new issues on GitHub or contacting us on Discord.

- **If you're a developer:** Use our contributing guide for developers to start. It provides information on setting up the development environment, coding standards, and the contribution process.
- **If you're interested in writing documentation:** Explore our documentation guidelines. These guidelines ensure that your documentation contributions align with our standards.

## Contribute to docs

Our documentation is a crucial part of Xata, and we welcome contributions from individuals who are passionate about clear and informative documentation. You can find our documentation source code on GitHub.
To maintain the quality in our documentation, we appreciate your help in identifying and addressing bugs and documentation gaps. If you encounter any issues while using Xata or reading our docs, don't hesitate to report them. Open a new issue in GitHub, providing as much detail as possible. This includes steps to reproduce the issue, expected behavior, and actual behavior. You can use the **Edit on GitHub** button located on each page underneath the page title or clone the repository. To learn more, refer to the docs [contributor guide](/docs/contribute/docs).

## Create content

Beyond code and documentation, you can contribute by creating valuable content. Share your expertise or experience using Xata by creating tutorials, blog posts, or other educational materials. Your content can help the community better understand and use Xata's capabilities.
By participating in the Xata community and making meaningful contributions, you not only enhance the platform but also have the opportunity to earn recognition.

We encourage you to explore the various avenues of contribution that align with your interests. Your contributions play a pivotal role in the growth and success of Xata. Join our community and collaborate to make Xata even better.

## Recognizing contributors

For those who wish to be recognized for their contributions, we acknowledge valuable contributions in our changelog, newsletter, and in Discord.
